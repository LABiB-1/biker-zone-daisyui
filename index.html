Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

To determine if your helmet needs replacement, check its age; helmets should generally be replaced every 3-5 years. Look for any cracks, dents, or significant scratches on the exterior. Inspect the inner foam lining for signs of compression or wear, and ensure the straps and buckles are in good condition. Ensure the helmet still fits comfortably and hasn't been involved in any crashes or significant impacts. Upgrading to a newer helmet with the latest safety standards is also a good idea if yours is outdated.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

If your bike has been in storage, inspect it for any rust, damage, or wear and ensure the tires, brakes, and gears are functioning properly. It's also a good idea to have it checked by a professional before riding.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Always wear a helmet, follow traffic laws, use hand signals, and stay visible with lights and reflective gear. Ride predictably, staying in bike lanes or to the right, and be aware of your surroundings.
